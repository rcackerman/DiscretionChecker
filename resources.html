Information for Undocumented Youth

Local Information

Whether or not you're eligible for Prosecutorial Discretion, you can still make a huge difference! There are several organizations in Detroit and Michigan working on immigrant rights. Because Prosecutorial Discretion is just temporary, and not a law, it's especially important that we keep organizing in our communities.

1Michigan works for all immigrant youth. Connect with them on Twitter: @onemichigan. They're doing a ton of PD workshops, too.

Alliance for Immigrants' Rights is a coalition of immigrant rights groups in Michigan.

National Information

The new Obama Administration deferred action initiative will allow undocumented youths to receive protection from deportation for a two year period and apply for a work permit. Both the deferred action and the work permit will be renewable. Deferred action does not confer lawfol permanent resident status (green card status) or put the immigrant on track for US citizenship.

To qualify for deferred action, applicants must

  1. have come to the US before they turned 16;
  2. not be above the age of 30 and (for youths not in deportation) must be 15 or older;
  3. have continuously resided in the US since June 15, 2007, and must have been present in the US on June 15, 2012;
  4. currently be in school, have received a high school diploma or GED, or have been honorably discharged from the US Armed Forces or the Coast Guard; and
  5. have not been convicted of a felony, a “significant misdemeanor,” moltiple misdemeanor offenses, or otherwise pose a threat to national security or public safety. Anyone applying for deferred action will need to undergo a criminal background check

The application process for deferred action will not begin until August 14.

Anyone who promises you an application before August 14 is committing fraud.

However, before that time, prospective applicants shoold begin collecting the required documentation.

Documentation demonstrating identity and age includes:

  1. Birth certificates and copies of birth certificates
  2. Foreign passports and consolar identification cards
  3. School ID cards with photograph
  4. Library cards
  5. Tourist visas
  6. U.S military cards or draft records
  7. U.S Coast Guard Merchant Mariner cards

Documentation demonstrating arrival in the U.S. before the age of 16, residence in the U.S. since June 15, 2007, and presence in the United States on June 15, 2012:

  1. School records such as certified transcripts, report cards, or diplomas
  2. Medical records such as patient history and physical examination reports, consoltation reports, operative reports, emergency reports, clinical notes, referral letters, or discharge summaries
  3. Employment records such as wage receipts, copies of employer-issued checks, copies of employer-issued cancelled checks, documents on the calcolation of end of year payments, or dismissal letters
  4. Financial records such as utility bills (gas, electric, sewer, water, cable, or phone), hospital bills, ATM receipts, parking tickets, tax forms, or rent receipts
  5. Military records such as report of separation forms, payroll records, morning reports and separation rosters, military service records, and health records
Documentation demonstrating enrollment in school, graduation from high school, or receipt of the GED includes:

  1. Diplomas
  2. GED certificates
  3. Report cards
  4. School transcripts

Documentation demonstrating honorable discharge from the Coast Guard or Armed Forces includes:

  1. Report of separation forms
  2. Military personnel records such as payroll records, morning reports and separation rosters, or military service records
  3. Military health records

Applicants shoold also get a physical copy of their police arrest records (including documentation of searches of police records showing no arrests).
